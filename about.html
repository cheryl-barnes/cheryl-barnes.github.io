about me

I was born and raised in California. I earned my BS in biology from San Diego State University, my MS in marine science from Moss Landing Marine Laboratories, and my PhD in fisheries from University of Alaska Fairbanks. I am generally interested in research that supports fishery sustainability and helps find solutions to resource management problems. I have also served as a program director in the nonprofit sector, program representative for California Sea Grant, and lead scientist for the California Collaborative Fisheries Research Program.

I am passionate about enhancing diversity, equity, and inclusion in science and wholeheartedly believe in the power of visibility. I am a cis woman, first generation college student from a working class family, identify as queer, and strive to be an ally to other underrepresented minorities in my field.
