cheryl barnes (she | her)
PC: M. Kosma

I am currently a post-doctoral fellow with NOAA Fisheries (the Alaska Fisheries Science Center and Alaska Regional Office) and the University of Washington. I use a variety of statistical models to evaluate biological, behavioral, and ecological responses to changing environmental conditions in Alaskan waters. My work has focused primarily on groundfishes in the eastern Pacific Ocean.

Generally, I am interested in spatially-explicit population and community dynamics and conducting research that can directly inform resource management. I value collaboration among academic and agency scientists, resource managers, fishery stakeholders, and students because of its benefits to both process and product.
